About

Data is not just technical. It is deeply human.

I didn't come from academia. I came from the floor. Building dashboards that nobody used, fixing pipelines that broke on Monday morning, and slowly figuring out what it actually takes to make data work inside a real organisation.

From Paris to Seoul to Geneva. Through Céline, The Kooples, L'Oréal, Cartier, and now Vacheron Constantin. I've learned that the hardest part of data is never the technology. It's the people, the politics, and the organisation around it.

I'm not looking to become a full-time academic. I want to bring the real thing into the classroom: the decisions, the trade-offs, the politics, and now the questions that AI is forcing every organisation to answer — whether they are ready or not.

Teaching

Five modules, one conviction.

I love my work. Always have. But one of the best parts of a career in data isn't the architecture or the pipelines. It's watching someone who never thought of themselves as "technical" realise they can read a data model, question an assumption, or challenge a number in a meeting.

I want to bring the industry into the room. Not in a way that reveals anything confidential, but in a way that makes students understand what data work actually looks like when the company has 150 years of history, global operations, and a board that still runs on intuition.

01 Data in Luxury and Fashion: What Makes It Different +

Same tools, different rules. Why standard data playbooks do not work in luxury.

  • Understand why luxury data is structurally different from mass-market data
  • Explore key use cases: clienteling, retail analytics, omnichannel identity
  • Navigate the tension between personalisation, privacy and the luxury customer relationship
  • Real examples across Cartier, Vacheron Constantin, Céline and L'Oréal
Guest lecture (1 to 3h) or semester module · All levels · No technical background required
02 Data Governance and Data Mesh +

Why data ownership fails. And how to fix it at the organisational level.

  • Understand why governance initiatives fail without the right ownership model
  • Evaluate trade-offs between centralised architecture and Data Mesh
  • Map an organisation's data landscape and identify where ownership breaks down
  • Apply frameworks to real luxury and fashion scenarios
Guest lecture (1 to 3h) or semester module · All levels · No technical background required
03 Building a Data Platform: The Manager's Perspective +

Your students will be asked to choose tools costing 200k per year. This module teaches them how, without being an engineer.

  • Understand what the Modern Data Stack is and how it evolved
  • Evaluate and justify data tools from a business standpoint, not a technical one
  • See how real platforms were built and evolved across luxury and fashion companies
  • Build the business case for a data infrastructure investment
Guest lecture (1 to 3h) or semester module · All levels · No technical background required
04 Data Culture and Change Management +

Why data projects fail. And how to make them stick.

  • Understand why 80% of data initiatives fail to deliver on their promise
  • Assess the data maturity of an organisation
  • Identify the levers that drive genuine adoption: sponsorship, quick wins, storytelling
  • Apply change frameworks to real transformation scenarios in luxury and fashion
Guest lecture (1 to 3h) or semester module · All levels · No technical background required
05 AI in the Enterprise: Reality, Governance and the Luxury Question +

Everyone is deploying AI. Few are governing it. Even fewer are doing it inside an industry where the brand is the product and the client relationship is everything.

  • Understand how AI is actually changing data work day to day: not the hype, the reality
  • See what AI governance looks like inside a real organisation, from policy to practice
  • Explore why luxury is a particularly complex case: client anonymity, brand sensitivity, the human relationship at the core
  • Discuss the questions organisations are not asking yet — but should be
Guest lecture (1 to 3h) or semester module . All levels . No technical background required
